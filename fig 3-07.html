Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

Introducing Mozart (January 27, 1756 – December 5, 1791), a genius Austrian composer born in Salzburg on January 27, 1756.

  • Life

  • Music

Life

Mozart was born in Salzburg on January 27, 1756. He learned piano and violin from his father, who was a court musician. He began composing at the age of five, and between 1764 and 1765, he learned how to compose symphonies from Bach, which led him to write many symphonies. In Vienna in 1784, Mozart met the 14-year-old Beethoven and devoted himself to teaching him.

Death

Mozart died suddenly of illness around 12:55 a.m. on December 5, 1791. The Requiem, which he was unable to complete, was finished by Franz Xaver Süssmayr.

Music

Mozart composed over 600 works in a wide variety of genres, including operas, symphonies, marches, orchestral dances, piano concertos, violin concertos, sacred choral music, cantatas, and masses, leaving a lasting legacy for future generations.

Legend Surrounding Mozart's Death

It is said that it rained and thundered on the day of Mozart's funeral, but according to the New Grove, it was actually a clear day without a single cloud.
